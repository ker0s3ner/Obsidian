## Type I — Infinite Limits

When one or both limits of integration are infinite, replace with a limit:

$$\int_a^{\infty} f(x),dx = \lim_{b\to\infty} \int_a^b f(x),dx$$

> [!example] $$\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2},dx = \lim_{b\to\infty}\left[-\frac{1}{x}\right]_1^b = 0 - (-1) = 1 \quad \checkmark \text{ convergent}$$

---

## Type II — Vertical Asymptote

When $f(x)$ is unbounded at a point inside or at the boundary of $[a, b]$:

$$\int_a^b f(x),dx = \lim_{t\to a^+} \int_t^b f(x),dx$$

> [!example] $$\int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}},dx = \lim_{t\to 0^+}\int_t^1 x^{-1/2},dx = \lim_{t\to 0^+}\left[2\sqrt{x}\right]_t^1 = 2 \quad \checkmark \text{ convergent}$$

---

## Divergent Example

$$\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x},dx = \lim_{b \to \infty} \ln b = \infty \quad \text{divergent}$$

---

## $p$-Test

$$\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^p},dx \begin{cases} \text{converges} & p > 1 \ \text{diverges} & p \leq 1 \end{cases}$$

---

## Comparison Test

If $0 \leq f(x) \leq g(x)$ on $[a, \infty)$:

|If|Then|
|---|---|
|$\int g$ converges|$\int f$ also converges|
|$\int f$ diverges|$\int g$ also diverges|

---

## Discontinuity Inside the Interval

If $f$ has an infinite discontinuity at $x = c \in (a, b)$, split:

$$\int_a^b f(x),dx = \lim_{t\to c^-}\int_a^t f,dx + \lim_{t\to c^+}\int_t^b f,dx$$

Both limits must converge for the integral to converge.

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_0^{\infty} e^{-x},dx$.
2. Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_0^1 \frac{1}{x^{1/3}},dx$.
3. Determine convergence: $\displaystyle\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^{3/2}},dx$.
4. Use the comparison test: does $\displaystyle\int_1^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2 + 1},dx$ converge?
5. Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_{-1}^{1} \frac{1}{x^2},dx$ — does it converge?
